About me

I will be joining the Department of Industrial and Systems Engineering at Kennesaw State University (KSU) as an Assistant Professor.

Before joining KSU, I worked as an Associate Data Scientist in the Department of Health Services Research at The University of Texas MD Anderson Cancer Center (MDACC). During my time at MDACC, I was awarded the Institute for Data Science in Oncology (IDSO) Fellowship, which provides training in applying data science to cancer care, prevention, research, and operations.

I am also a member of the National Cancer Institute-sponsored Cancer Intervention and Surveillance Modeling Network (CISNET) Lung Working Group.

Prior to that, I was a postdoctoral fellow in the Industrial and Systems Engineering Department at Wayne State University (2022–2024). I received the Postdoctoral Trainee Research Award from the Postdoctoral Office at the Graduate School of Wayne State University for two consecutive years (2023–2024).

Before moving to the United States, I served as an Assistant Professor in the Industrial Engineering Department at Alzahra University in Tehran, Iran (2021–2022).

I received my Ph.D. in Industrial Engineering from the University of Tehran in 2018, where I also completed a one-year postdoctoral fellowship (2019–2020), supported by an award from Iran's National Elites Foundation.

During my PhD program, I awarded a research grant from the International Affairs Department of the University of Tehran to serve as a visiting researcher in the Department of Industrial Engineering at the University of Pittsburgh (2016-2017).

I received my M.S. and B.S. degrees in Industrial Engineering from K. N. Toosi University of Technology in Tehran, Iran, in 2010 and 2012, respectively.

Research Interests

My research sits at the intersection of methodological innovation and real-world application. On the methodological side, I focus on decision analytics, sequential decision-making under uncertainty (Markov Decision Process, Partially Observable Markov Decision Process), multi-objective optimization, simulation, and machine learning. On the application side, I am interested in health systems, medical decision-making — especially in cancer care and diabetes management — and logistics.
