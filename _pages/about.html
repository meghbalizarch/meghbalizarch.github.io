Welcome to the Health Systems Optimization (HSOpt) Lab

The Health Systems Optimization (HSOpt) Lab at Kennesaw State University integrates systems engineering and data science to enhance healthcare delivery and management, optimizing operations to achieve better patient outcomes and overall efficiency. Our work focuses on the model-based approaches for safe and efficient delivery of care through operations and process engineering. Using Markov modeling, simulation, optimization, predictive analytics, and multi-criteria decision analysis, we develop solutions to support medical decision-making and analyze the cost-effectiveness of healthcare interventions.

Dr. Eghbalizarch is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Industrial and Systems Engineering (ISYE) at the Southern Polytechnic College of Engineering and Engineering Technology (SPCEET), Kennesaw State University (KSU). She is an industrial engineer whose work focuses on improving healthcare delivery through advanced modeling and analytics. Her research integrates simulation, optimization, and data science to support decision-making in healthcare systems. She is a member of the National Cancer Institute-sponsored Cancer Intervention and Surveillance Modeling Network (CISNET), the Society for Medical Decision Making (SMDM), the Institute of Operations Research and the Management Science (INFORMS), and the Institute of Industrial and Systems Engineers (IISE).

Research Interests

Her research sits at the intersection of methodological innovation and real-world application. On the methodological side, she focuses on sequential decision-making under uncertainty (Markov Decision Process, Partially Observable Markov Decision Process), multi-objective optimization, simulation, data analytics and machine learning. On the application side, she is interested in health systems, medical decision-making — especially in cancer care and diabetes management — and logistics.

Bio

Before joining KSU, she worked as an Associate Data Scientist in the Department of Health Services Research at The University of Texas MD Anderson Cancer Center (MDACC). During her time at MDACC, she was awarded the Institute for Data Science in Oncology (IDSO) Fellowship, which provides training in applying data science to cancer care, prevention, research, and operations.

She is also a member of the National Cancer Institute-sponsored Cancer Intervention and Surveillance Modeling Network (CISNET) Lung Working Group.

Prior to that, she was a postdoctoral fellow in the Industrial and Systems Engineering Department at Wayne State University (2022–2024). She received the Postdoctoral Trainee Research Award from the Postdoctoral Office at the Graduate School of Wayne State University for two consecutive years (2023–2024).

Before moving to the United States, she served as an Assistant Professor in the Industrial Engineering Department at Alzahra University in Tehran, Iran (2021–2022).

She received her Ph.D. in Industrial Engineering from the University of Tehran in 2018, where she also completed a one-year postdoctoral fellowship (2019–2020), supported by an award from Iran's National Elites Foundation.

During her PhD program, she was awarded a research grant from the International Affairs Department of the University of Tehran to serve as a visiting researcher in the Department of Industrial Engineering at the University of Pittsburgh (2016-2017).

She received her M.S. and B.S. degrees in Industrial Engineering from K. N. Toosi University of Technology in Tehran, Iran, in 2010 and 2012, respectively.
